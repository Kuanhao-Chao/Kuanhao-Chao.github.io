Kuan-Hao Chao

趙冠豪 is my Chinese name. My first name consists of two words, Kuan (冠) and Hao (豪), connected with a hyphen. Kuan (冠) means 'crown', and it can also be used as a verb describing the action of 'putting A on top of B', which implies 'being a champion'. Hao (豪) means 'big' and 'grand', signifying 'having the characteristics of a hero'. When combined, Kuan-Hao (冠豪) represents the love and expectations my parents have for me. My last name is Chao (趙), and an interesting fact is that Chao was the surname of 18 emperors from the ancient and prosperous Song Dynasty in China (A.D. 960 - A.D. 1279).
